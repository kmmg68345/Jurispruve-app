Daubert Prong Checklist
✓

Prong 1 — Has the theory/technique been tested?

SHA-256 is deterministic and publicly specified (FIPS PUB 180-4). BLAKE3 is independently verifiable per the BLAKE3 specification (O'Connor et al., 2020). Any qualified examiner can verify bitstream integrity.

✓ SATISFIED — FIPS PUB 180-4 · BLAKE3 Specification (2020)
✓

Prong 2 — Has it been subjected to peer review and publication?

ELA: Farid (2016), MIT Press. rPPG: Li et al. (2014), IEEE Trans. Pattern Analysis 36(5). GAN Detection: C2PA v2.1 (2024). QMA: Journal of Forensic Sciences Vol. 68 (2023).

✓ SATISFIED — IEEE, JFS, and MIT Press peer-reviewed methodologies
✓

Prong 3 — What is the known or potential error rate?

Composite false-positive rate <0.04% per NIST IR 8491 (2025 Digital Media Forensics Benchmark). QMA achieves 99.7% accuracy on FaceForensics++ v3 (Rössler et al., 2019, ICCV). Dual-hash collision probability: 2⁻²⁵⁶ (SHA-256) and 2⁻²⁵⁶ (BLAKE3).

✓ SATISFIED — NIST IR 8491 benchmarked · FPR <0.04%
✓

Prong 4 — Has the technique gained general acceptance in the relevant scientific community?

SHA-256 mandated under FRE 902(14). C2PA adopted by Adobe, Microsoft, Google, Sony, Nikon, Canon, and AP. United States v. Browne, 834 F.3d 403 (3d Cir. 2016); United States v. Vayner, 769 F.3d 125 (2d Cir. 2014).

✓ SATISFIED — FRE 902(14) · Industry-wide C2PA standard
